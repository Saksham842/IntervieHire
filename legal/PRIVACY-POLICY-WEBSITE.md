## 7. Automated decision-making, AI & explainability

The Services use AI to **score and assess** your interview answers (and, where provided, your résumé)
against a role-specific rubric. We aim to be transparent about it:

- **AI assists; a human decides.** AI outputs are **decision support** — by themselves they do **not**
  accept, reject, rank, or filter candidates. A human recruiter reviews the results and makes the
  hiring decision; advancing or rejecting a candidate is always a **manual human action**.
- **How the AI evaluates you (explainability).** The AI scores **only the content of what you say** —
  the text of your answers, against the role's required skills and a rubric set by the Client/role. For
  each answer it produces a structured evaluation across defined dimensions (such as alignment to the
  model answer, factual correctness, concept coverage, reasoning quality, technical specificity, and
  clarity/structure), the rubric points it found evidence for, plus overall and per-skill scores,
  strengths, growth areas, and any integrity red-flags. **It does not use your facial expressions,
  gaze, voice characteristics, appearance, or any protected characteristic (such as race, gender, or
  age) to score you.**
- **Fairness.** Every candidate is assessed against the **same role rubric**, we do **not** collect
  demographic data to score you, and we test our automated evaluations for bias and adverse impact.
- **Proctoring is separate from scoring.** Camera/voice integrity monitoring is used only to flag
  possible anomalies for the recruiter's review — it does **not** grade your skills.
- **Your right to human review.** You may **obtain human intervention, express your point of view, and
  contest** an AI-assisted decision (GDPR Art. 22(3)). Contact **[DPO EMAIL]**, or the Client you
  interviewed with (who makes the final decision).
- **AI-generated avatar.** Where an AI avatar conducts your interview, it is **artificially generated**
  and is labelled as AI-generated, as required by India's IT Rules (synthetic-content amendment, 2025).

We implement reasonable technical and organisational safeguards, including encryption in transit and at

## 14. Children, vulnerable persons & accessibility

The Services are **intended for individuals aged 18 and over.**

- We ask candidates to confirm they are **18 or older** before a camera-based interview.
- Under the DPDP Act and DPDP Rules 2025, processing the data of a person **under 18**, or of a **person
  with a disability who has a lawful guardian**, requires **verifiable parental/guardian consent**. We
  do not knowingly process such data without it; if you believe we hold a minor's data without proper
  consent, contact us and we will delete it.
- Under the GDPR/UK GDPR, additional protections apply to children's data.

**Accessibility & accommodations.** If you need an adjustment to take the interview — for example due to
a disability, or if you prefer not to use a camera — you may take a **text-only interview** where the
role permits, or contact **[GRIEVANCE/DPO EMAIL]** (or the Client) and we will work with you on a
reasonable accommodation.
