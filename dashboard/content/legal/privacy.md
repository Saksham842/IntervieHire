## 1. Our two roles (please read — it determines your rights)

IntervieHire processes personal data in **two distinct capacities**, and which one applies changes
who is legally responsible:

- **As a Controller / Data Fiduciary.** When we decide why and how data is processed — for example,
  for visitors to our website, recruiter account holders, our public demo interview, candidates who
  apply through a public/direct IntervieHire link, and individuals we surface through our sourcing
  ("Talent Finder") feature — **we are responsible** and this Policy governs.
- **As a Processor / Data Processor.** When a recruiter or employer (our customer, the
  "**Client**") uses IntervieHire to interview and assess *their* candidates, the **Client is the
  Controller/Data Fiduciary** and decides the purposes. We process that candidate data **on the
  Client's instructions** under a Data Processing Agreement. In that case, the **Client's** privacy
  notice governs, and candidates should direct requests to the Client (we will assist the Client).

If you are unsure which applies to you, contact us and we will tell you.

## 2. Definitions

- **Personal data / personal information** — any information relating to an identified or
  identifiable natural person.
- **Special category data (GDPR) / sensitive data (India, until 13 May 2027 under the SPDI Rules
  2011)** — includes **biometric data**. Our face/gaze analysis and voice recordings are treated as
  biometric data and given the highest protection.
- **Processing** — any operation performed on personal data (collection, storage, use, sharing,
  deletion, etc.).
- **Sub-processor** — a third party we engage to process personal data on our behalf.
- **You** — a candidate, recruiter, website visitor, or other individual whose data we process.

## 7. Automated decision-making, AI & explainability (important)

The Services use AI to **score and assess** your interview answers (and, where provided, your résumé)
against a role-specific rubric. We want this to be transparent:

- **AI assists; a human decides.** AI outputs are **decision support** — by themselves they do **not**
  accept, reject, rank, or filter candidates. A human recruiter reviews the results and makes the
  hiring decision; advancing or rejecting a candidate is always a **manual human action**.
- **How the AI evaluates you (explainability).** The AI scores **only the content of what you say** —
  the text of your answers, assessed against the role's required skills and a scoring rubric set by the
  Client/role. For each answer it produces a structured evaluation across defined dimensions (such as
  alignment to the model answer, factual correctness, concept coverage, reasoning quality, technical
  specificity, and clarity/structure), noting the rubric points it found evidence for, plus overall and
  per-skill scores, strengths, growth areas, and any integrity "red flags." **The competency scoring
  does not use your facial expressions, gaze, voice characteristics, appearance, or any protected
  characteristic** (such as race, gender, or age).
- **Proctoring is separate from scoring.** Camera/voice **integrity monitoring** (proctoring) is used
  only to flag possible anomalies for the recruiter's review — it does **not** grade your skills.
- **Fairness.** Every candidate is assessed against the **same role rubric**, and we do **not** collect
  demographic data (such as race, gender, or age) to score you.
- **Your right to human review.** You have the right to **obtain human intervention, express your point
  of view, and contest** an AI-assisted decision (GDPR Art. 22(3)). To exercise this, contact us at
  **[DPO EMAIL]**, or the Client you interviewed with (who makes the final decision).
- **AI-generated avatar.** Where an AI avatar conducts your interview, it is **artificially generated**
  and is labelled as AI-generated, as required by India's IT Rules (synthetic-content amendment, 2025).

## 11. How we protect your data (security)

We implement **reasonable technical and organisational safeguards**, including encryption in transit
and at rest, access controls and access logging, least-privilege access, backups, and breach
response procedures. In the event of a personal-data breach we will notify the relevant authority and affected
individuals as required. Under the **DPDP Rules 2025**, we will inform **affected Data Principals
without undue delay** and the **Data Protection Board of India without delay**, followed by a
**detailed report to the Board within 72 hours** (extendable on the Board's permission). Under the
**GDPR**, we will notify the competent supervisory authority **within 72 hours** of becoming aware
where the breach is likely to risk individuals' rights, and affected individuals without undue delay
where that risk is high.

No system is perfectly secure; we cannot guarantee absolute security.

## 14. Children and vulnerable persons

The Services are **intended for individuals aged 18 and over.**

- We ask candidates to confirm they are **18 or older** before a camera-based interview.
- Under the DPDP Act and DPDP Rules 2025, processing the data of a person **under 18**, or of a
  **person with a disability who has a lawful guardian**, requires **verifiable parental/guardian
  consent**. We do not knowingly process such data without it. If you believe we hold a minor's data
  without proper consent, contact us and we will delete it.
- Under the GDPR/UK GDPR, additional protections apply to children's data.

**Accessibility & accommodations.** If you need an adjustment to take the interview — for example due to
a disability — contact **[GRIEVANCE/DPO EMAIL]** or the Client you are interviewing with, and we will
work with you and the Client on a reasonable accommodation where possible.
